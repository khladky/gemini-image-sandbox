# example.txt — example Gemini queries for the Flickr Gemini Sandbox
#
# Copy any of these into the query box to try them out.

---

BASIC DESCRIPTION:
Describe this photo in three to four sentences.

---

TITLE AND DESCRIPTION:
Generate a short Flickr photo title and a three to four sentence description.

---

SARCASTIC TOUR GUIDE:
Describe this photo as if narrated by a dry, world-weary tour guide who has seen it all a thousand times and is no longer impressed by anything, but still finds it mildly amusing to comment on.

---

PHOTO CRITIQUE:
Give a critical analysis of this photo as if written by a demanding art critic. Comment on composition, lighting, subject matter, and overall impact.

---

DRY TECHNICAL:
Describe this photo as if written by a methodical forensic analyst — precise, factual, and emotionally detached. Note composition, lighting conditions, colour palette, and any technically notable elements.

---

POETIC:
Write a short poetic description of this photo with rich sensory imagery and a contemplative tone. Favour concrete specific details over abstract emotional statements.

---

FIVE YEAR OLD:
Describe this photo as a five-year-old child would, using very simple words, lots of excitement, and the occasional charmingly wrong guess about what is happening.

---

IDENTIFY LOCATION:
Based on any visual clues in this photo, can you identify or make an educated guess about where it was taken? Explain your reasoning.

---

TAG SUGGESTIONS:
Suggest 15 to 20 Flickr tags for this photo. Reply with only a comma-separated list, all lowercase, multi-word tags hyphenated.

---

IDENTIFY OBJECTS:
List all the significant objects, people, animals, and landmarks visible in this photo.

---

MOOD AND ATMOSPHERE:
Describe the mood and atmosphere of this photo. What emotions does it evoke and why?

---

STORY:
Write a short two to three paragraph fictional story inspired by this photo.

---

SARCASTIC TOUR GUIDE:
Generate a short, natural Flickr photo title and a sarcastic five-to-seven sentence description. Write the description as if narrated by a dry, world-weary tour guide who has seen it all a thousand times and is no longer impressed by anything, but still finds it mildly amusing to comment on. Include at least one deadpan or ironic observation.

---

DRY TECHNICAL ANALYST:
Generate a short, natural Flickr photo title and a dry, technical four-to-five sentence description. Write as if narrated by a methodical photographic equipment reviewer or forensic analyst — precise, factual, and emotionally detached. Note specific visual details such as composition, lighting conditions, colour palette, and any technically notable elements, without any subjective commentary, enthusiasm, or attempt at humour.

---

CONTEMPLATIVE POET:
Generate a short, evocative Flickr photo title and a poetic three-to-four sentence description. Write with rich, sensory imagery and a contemplative tone, as if narrated by a thoughtful poet observing the scene. Favour concrete, specific sensory details over abstract emotional statements. Avoid clichés, avoid overused poetic phrases like "stands as a testament to" or "a reminder of", and avoid being overly flowery — restraint and precision matter more than ornamentation.

---

FIVE-YEAR-OLD CHILD:
Generate a short, simple Flickr photo title and a three-to-four sentence description written exactly as a five-year-old child would say it — very simple short words, basic grammar, lots of excitement and wonder, maybe a few run-on sentences, and the occasional charmingly wrong fact or guess about what's happening. Do not use any sophisticated vocabulary.

---

FRENCH (or substitute any language):
Generate a short, natural Flickr photo title and a three-to-four sentence description, written in French.

---

CRITICAL:
Generate a short, natural Flickr photo title and a five-to-seven sentence critique of the image. Critisize both the technical and the esthetic aspects of the image. Be harsh, honest and completely unforgiving.

---

VULGAR :
Generate a short Flickr photo title and a three-to-four sentence description written in a crude, sweary, irreverent voice — like a foul-mouthed mate describing the scene down the pub.
